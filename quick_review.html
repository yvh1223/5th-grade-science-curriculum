🔬 1. How Scientists Work TEKS 5.1–5.4

The Scientific Method (memorize this order)

  1. Question — What do I want to find out?
  2. Hypothesis — An "If…, then…" educated guess you can test.
  3. Experiment — Test ONE thing at a time. Keep all other conditions the same.
  4. Data — Write down measurements (numbers, observations).
  5. Conclusion — Does the data support the hypothesis? Why or why not?

Variables (almost guaranteed on the test)

Independent variable: what YOU change (the cause).
Dependent variable: what you MEASURE (the effect).
Controlled variables: what stays the SAME so the test is fair.
💡 Trick: The independent variable is listed first in "Does ___ affect ___?" The dependent variable is what comes after "affect".

Graph types

  • Line graph — shows CHANGE OVER TIME (plant growth, temperature).
  • Bar graph — COMPARES categories (favorite foods, amounts).
  • Pie chart — shows PARTS of a whole (percentages).
⚠️ Common trap: If a question shows data over time, the answer is LINE graph. If it's comparing separate things, it's BAR graph.

🧪 2. Matter TEKS 5.5

Physical Properties (use these to identify matter)

Mass: amount of matter (grams).
Volume: space matter takes up (mL or cm³).
Density: mass ÷ volume. Heavy for its size = denser. Denser stuff SINKS.
Magnetism: attracted to magnets (iron, nickel, cobalt only).
Conductivity: lets heat or electricity pass through (metals do, wood/plastic don't).
Solubility: can it dissolve in water (salt yes, sand no).
State at room temp: solid, liquid, or gas.
Melting/freezing/boiling point: temperatures where state changes.

Physical vs Chemical Change (CRITICAL)

Physical ChangeChemical Change
Same substance, new shape/state.
Examples: melting, freezing, cutting, dissolving salt, crumpling paper.		 NEW substance is made.
Examples: burning, rusting, cooking an egg, baking a cake, vinegar + baking soda.
🔑 Signs of a CHEMICAL change: color change, gas/bubbles that weren't from boiling, light or heat released, unusual smell, solid forms from liquids, change can't be undone.

Conservation of Mass

Mass is ALWAYS conserved. In a sealed container, mass before = mass after, even during chemical reactions.

⚠️ Trap: If a flask LOSES mass after a reaction, a gas escaped. Mass wasn't destroyed — it flew away as gas.

Mixtures & Solutions

  • Mixture: two or more things combined; each keeps its properties. Can be separated physically.
  • Solution: a mixture where one thing dissolves evenly into another (salt water, lemonade).
  • Separation tricks: magnet (iron), filter (sand from water), evaporation (salt from water), sifting (big vs small bits).

⚡ 3. Energy & Force TEKS 5.6

Forms of Energy (know ALL of these)

Mechanical: motion or position (wind, moving car).
Light: from Sun, bulbs, fire.
Heat (thermal): from fire, friction, Sun.
Sound: vibrations traveling as waves.
Electrical: flowing charge in circuits.
Chemical: stored in food, fuel, batteries.
Nuclear: stored in atoms (Sun, power plants).
Kinetic: energy of motion (moving ball).
Potential: stored energy from position (ball at top of hill).

Energy Transformations (common STAAR questions)

DeviceEnergy transformation
FlashlightChemical → Electrical → Light + Heat
Solar panelLight → Electrical

Plant photosynthesisLight → Chemical (stored in sugar)
🔑 Law of Conservation of Energy: Energy can't be created or destroyed — only changed from one form to another. Some always becomes heat (that's why devices get warm).

Electric Circuits

  • Closed circuit = complete loop = electricity flows = bulb lights.
  • Open circuit = broken loop = no flow = bulb off.
  • Conductors let electricity flow (metal wires, water with salt).
  • Insulators block electricity (rubber, plastic, wood, glass).
  • Series circuit: one path. Remove a bulb → all bulbs go out.
  • Parallel circuit: multiple paths. Remove a bulb → others stay on.

Light & Sound

  • Light travels in straight lines. It can be reflected (mirror), refracted (bent through water), or absorbed.
  • Shadows form when light is blocked. Closer to light = bigger shadow.
  • Sound needs a medium (air, water, solid). Sound CAN'T travel through empty space.
  • Louder = more energy put in. Higher pitch = faster vibrations.

Force & Motion

  • Force = a push or pull (measured in Newtons, N).
  • Bigger force OR less mass → more change in motion.
  • Gravity pulls things DOWN toward Earth's center.
  • Friction slows things down and makes heat.
  • Balanced forces = no change in motion. Unbalanced forces = something speeds up, slows down, or changes direction.

🌍 4. Earth & Space TEKS 5.7, 5.8

Water Cycle (learn these 4 words)

  1. Evaporation — Sun heats water → it becomes water vapor (gas) and rises.
  2. Condensation — vapor cools in the sky → tiny droplets form clouds.
  3. Precipitation — droplets grow heavy → fall as rain, snow, sleet, or hail.
  4. Collection / Runoff — water collects in oceans, lakes, rivers, or seeps into the ground (groundwater).
🔑 Sun is the engine of the water cycle. Without the Sun, no evaporation.

Weather vs Climate

  • Weather = what's happening RIGHT NOW (today's rain).
  • Climate = average weather over MANY YEARS (Texas is mostly hot).

Clouds

  • Cumulus — puffy, fair-weather clouds (like cotton).
  • Stratus — flat, gray layers; often drizzle.
  • Cirrus — wispy, high, made of ice crystals.
  • Cumulonimbus — tall thunderstorm clouds.

Earth's Layers (outside → inside)

  1. Crust — thin, solid rock we live on.
  2. Mantle — hot, thick, slowly flowing rock.
  3. Outer core — LIQUID iron and nickel.
  4. Inner core — SOLID iron, extremely hot and under pressure.

Landforms & Erosion

  • Weathering breaks rocks into smaller pieces.
  • Erosion MOVES those pieces (by water, wind, ice, gravity).
  • Deposition drops them in a new place (deltas, beaches, sand dunes).
  • Fast-moving water carries more and bigger rocks than slow water.

Renewable vs Nonrenewable Resources

RenewableNonrenewable
Sun, wind, water, trees (if replanted)Coal, oil, natural gas, most minerals

Solar System (memorize order!)

My Very Educated Mother Just Served Us Nachos = Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune.

  • Inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars) — small, rocky.
  • Outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune) — big, gas/ice giants.
  • The farther from the Sun → the LONGER the year (orbital period).
  • The Sun is a star, the center of our solar system.

Day/Night, Seasons, Moon Phases

  • Day/Night: Earth rotates on its axis every 24 hours.
  • Seasons: caused by Earth's tilt (23.5°), NOT by distance from the Sun.
  • Year: Earth revolves around the Sun every 365¼ days.
  • Moon phases cycle: New → Waxing Crescent → First Quarter → Waxing Gibbous → Full → Waning Gibbous → Last Quarter → Waning Crescent → New. (≈29.5 days)
  • Waxing = getting bigger. Waning = getting smaller.
⚠️ Common trap: Seasons are NOT caused by Earth being closer or farther from the Sun. They're caused by Earth's TILT.

🌱 5. Living Things TEKS 5.9, 5.10

Food Chains & Webs

  • Producer — makes its own food (plants, algae). Uses photosynthesis.
  • Consumer — eats other living things.
    • Herbivore — eats only plants (rabbit, deer).
    • Carnivore — eats only animals (lion, hawk).
    • Omnivore — eats both (bear, human).
  • Decomposer — breaks down dead matter (bacteria, fungi, worms). Recycles nutrients back into soil.
  • Energy flows: Sun → Producer → Consumer → Decomposer.
  • Arrows in food chains point to the EATER (from food to animal).
🔑 10% Rule: Only about 10% of energy passes from one level to the next. That's why there are lots of plants, fewer herbivores, and very few top predators.

Photosynthesis

Plants take in sunlight + water + carbon dioxide → they make sugar (food) + oxygen.

Formula: Light + CO₂ + H₂O → Sugar (glucose) + O₂

Leaves catch light, roots take up water, tiny holes (stomata) take in CO₂.

Adaptations

  • An adaptation is a trait that helps an organism survive in its environment.
  • Physical adaptations: thick fur (cold), cactus spines (protection + less water loss), webbed feet (swimming), camouflage.
  • Behavioral adaptations: migration, hibernation, hunting in packs.

Inherited vs Learned

  • Inherited — from parents (eye color, fur color, bird beak shape).
  • Learned — practiced (riding a bike, dogs doing tricks, language).

Life Cycles

  • Complete metamorphosis (4 stages): egg → larva → pupa → adult. Butterflies, frogs, beetles.
  • Incomplete metamorphosis (3 stages): egg → nymph → adult. Grasshoppers, cockroaches.
  • Flowering plant: seed → seedling → adult → flowers → pollination → seeds.

Ecosystems

  • Biotic = living parts (plants, animals, bacteria).
  • Abiotic = non-living parts (rocks, water, air, sunlight, temperature).
  • A change in any one part affects the whole ecosystem.
  • Invasive species (like kudzu, zebra mussels) outcompete natives and damage ecosystems.
⚠️ Trap: If a producer dies out, EVERY level above dies too (no food at the base). If a top predator dies out, prey populations EXPLODE, then overgraze producers.

🎯 6. STAAR Test-Day Strategy

  1. Read each question twice. Circle key words like "EXCEPT", "NOT", "BEST", "MOST".
  2. Cross out wrong answers. Usually 2 of 4 are obviously wrong — eliminate them first.
  3. Look at the data FIRST on data/graph questions, then read the question.
  4. Watch for absolutes. Answers with "always" or "never" are often wrong.
  5. If stuck, flag it and move on. Come back with fresh eyes at the end.
  6. Eat breakfast. Sleep well. Pee before starting. 🙂
  7. Don't leave anything blank. A guess has a 25% chance; a blank is 0%.
  8. Use the whole time. If you finish early, re-check flagged questions and data problems.
