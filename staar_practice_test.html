STAAR Science Practice Test - Texas Official Format

Grade 5 - 32 Questions

Question 1: STAAR: Scientific Investigation

A student wants to investigate whether the amount of sunlight affects the growth rate of bean plants. She plants 12 identical bean seeds in identical pots with the same soil and water. She places 4 pots in full sunlight, 4 pots in partial shade, and 4 pots in complete darkness. After 3 weeks, she measures plant height. Which statement best describes the design of this experiment?

Question 3: STAAR: Measurement

A scientist measures the mass of a sample five times and records: 24.3g, 24.5g, 24.2g, 24.4g, and 24.3g. What is the best estimate of the sample's actual mass, and why is this method more reliable?

Question 4: STAAR: Experimental Design

Students test different materials to determine which is the best insulator to keep ice from melting. They wrap identical ice cubes in different materials (foil, cotton, foam, plastic) and measure how long each takes to melt completely. Which factor should be kept constant (controlled) in this experiment?

Question 5: STAAR: Models

A student designs a model of the water cycle using a clear plastic box, water, soil, and a heat lamp. She observes water evaporating, condensing on the lid, and dripping back down. What is the primary limitation of this model?

Question 6: STAAR: Graphing

Scientists studying plant growth record the following data for Plant A: Week 1: 8cm, Week 2: 12cm, Week 3: 18cm, Week 4: 26cm. Which type of graph would best display this information to show the pattern of continuous growth over time?

Question 7: STAAR: Properties of Matter

A student places a sealed balloon with a mass of 3 grams in a freezer. After several hours, the balloon shrinks but its mass is still 3 grams. Which statement best explains this observation?

Question 8: STAAR: Chemical Changes

Students mix baking soda (solid) with vinegar (liquid) in a flask. Bubbles form and the mixture gets colder. The students notice the flask now weighs less than before. What best explains the decrease in mass?

Question 9: STAAR: Physical vs Chemical

Which of the following observations provides the strongest evidence that a chemical change (not just a physical change) has occurred?

Question 10: STAAR: Mixtures

A student has a mixture of sand, salt, and iron filings. She wants to separate the mixture into its three components. Which procedure would successfully separate all three materials?

Question 11: STAAR: Energy

A metal ball rolling down a ramp has kinetic energy (energy of motion). At which point on the ramp does the ball have the GREATEST kinetic energy?

Question 12: STAAR: Thermal Energy

Two identical pots contain water at 25°C. Pot A contains 500 mL and Pot B contains 1000 mL. Both are heated until water temperature reaches 75°C. Which statement is correct about the heat energy required?

Question 13: STAAR: Heat Transfer

A student observes that a metal spoon feels colder than a wooden spoon when both are at room temperature (20°C). Why does the metal spoon feel colder even though both are the same temperature?

Question 14: STAAR: Properties of Water

Water has a higher specific heat capacity than sand. On a sunny beach day, which observation would you expect, and what does this tell us about water's properties?

Question 15: STAAR: Energy Transformation

A battery-powered flashlight converts chemical energy stored in batteries into light energy. Which other form of energy is also produced during this energy transformation?

Question 16: STAAR: Solar Energy

Solar panels on a house convert sunlight into electrical energy that powers lights and appliances. On a cloudy day, the panels produce less electricity than on a sunny day. What is the best explanation for this difference?

Question 17: STAAR: Wind Energy

A windmill converts wind energy (kinetic energy of moving air) into electrical energy. The windmill's blades spin faster when wind speed increases. What can be concluded about the relationship between wind speed and electrical energy produced?

Question 18: STAAR: Sound Energy

A student strikes a drum, causing it to vibrate and produce sound. She notices that striking the drum harder produces a louder sound. What form of energy is converted into sound energy when the drum is struck?

Question 19: STAAR: Hydroelectric Energy

A hydroelectric dam converts the potential energy of water stored behind the dam into electrical energy. As water falls through the dam, it spins turbines connected to generators. Which energy transformation occurs in this process?

Question 20: STAAR: Energy Efficiency

A student designs an energy-efficient house that uses solar panels, wind turbines, and insulation to reduce energy consumption. Which statement best explains why energy efficiency is important?

Question 21: STAAR: Earth's Structure

Scientists study Earth's interior using seismic waves from earthquakes. They discovered that S-waves (shear waves) cannot pass through Earth's outer core, but P-waves (pressure waves) can. What important conclusion did scientists make from this evidence?

Question 23: STAAR: Moon Phases

A student observes the Moon on four consecutive nights and records these observations: Night 1: thin crescent on right. Night 2: thicker crescent. Night 3: first quarter (right half lit). Night 4: gibbous (more than half lit). If this pattern continues, what phase should appear on Night 7?

Question 24: STAAR: Planetary Temperatures

Mars and Earth are both rocky planets with similar day lengths (Mars: 24.6 hours, Earth: 24 hours). However, Mars is much colder than Earth with an average temperature of -60°C compared to Earth's +15°C. What is the primary reason for this temperature difference?

Question 25: STAAR: Earth's Rotation

The Sun appears to move across the sky from east to west each day. However, this is an apparent motion caused by Earth's rotation. Which observation provides evidence that Earth rotates on its axis rather than the Sun moving around Earth?

Question 26: STAAR: Planetary Science

Scientists have discovered ice deposits at the poles of Mars and evidence of ancient river valleys. What hypothesis do these observations support about Mars' past environment?

Question 27: STAAR: Food Chains

In a food chain: Grass → Grasshopper → Frog → Snake → Hawk. A disease kills most grasshoppers. Which prediction about the other populations is most accurate?

Question 28: STAAR: Energy Pyramids

The diagram shows energy flow in an ecosystem: 10,000 grass plants → 1,000 grasshoppers → 100 frogs → 10 hawks. Why does the number of organisms decrease at each level of this energy pyramid?

Question 29: STAAR: Adaptations

Arctic foxes have thick white fur in winter that changes to thin brown fur in summer. How do these adaptations increase the fox's chances of survival?

Question 30: STAAR: Classification

A student classifies organisms into groups based on their characteristics. She places a mushroom in a different group from a fern plant. Which characteristic best justifies this classification decision?

Question 31: STAAR: Invasive Species

Kudzu is a fast-growing vine introduced to the United States from Asia. It grows rapidly, covering and killing native plants and trees. Why is kudzu considered an invasive species, and what impact does it have on native ecosystems?

Question 32: STAAR: Decomposers
